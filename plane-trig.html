Spherical Excess, Defect & Bounds

🔄 Click to Flip

Excess Metric & Fundamental Bounds

E = A + B + C - 180°
D = 360° - (a + b + c)

Core Rules for Sides and Angles:

  • The total interior angle sum must conform to: 180° < A+B+C < 540°.
  • The sum of all three angular sides is less than 360°.
  • The sum of any two sides is greater than the third side. Greater side sits opposite greater angle.

Napier's Rules: Sin-Co-Op

🔄 Click to Flip

The "Sin-Co-Op" Rule

sin(Middle) = cos(Opp₁) × cos(Opp₂)

Definition: The sine of any middle part is equal to the product of the cosines of its two opposite geometric parts.

  • Circular layout order: a, b, co-A, co-c, co-B.
  • The suffix "co-" means complement (e.g., 90° - Part).

The "Sin-Ta-Ad" Rule

sin(Middle) = tan(Adj₁) × tan(Adj₂)

Definition: The sine of any middle part is equal to the product of the tangents of its two adjacent geometric parts.

  • Parts are considered adjacent if they lie immediately next to the selected middle element in the 5-part loop.
  • Omit right angle C entirely from the cycle setup.
